4.A Job contract problem

Boss's profit is dependent on Tom's effort:

  • Tom's effort: e∈{H,L}e \in \{H,L\}e∈{H,L}.
  • Profit to boss: π∼Fe on [0,1]\pi \sim F_e \ on \ [0,1]π∼Fe​ on [0,1]
  • FHF_HFH​ first order stochastically dominates (FOSD) FLF_LFL​.
    • Pr(π≥x∣H)≥Pr(π≥x∣L)Pr(\pi \geq x | H) \geq Pr(\pi \geq x | L)Pr(π≥x∣H)≥Pr(π≥x∣L) for any x∈[0,1]x \in [0,1]x∈[0,1].
    • High effort is more likely to generate high profit.

Game:

  • Boss makes the job offer. The job contract will specify:
    1. Effort level eee that Tom has to exert.
    2. Wage w(π)w(\pi)w(π) that Tom will receive given π∈[0,1]\pi \in [0,1]π∈[0,1] (w(π:[0,1]→R+)w(\pi:[0,1] \to \mathbb{R}_+)w(π:[0,1]→R+​)).
  • Tom decides whether to accept or not.

Payoffs:

  • Boss's payoff: π−w\pi - wπ−w
    • Boss is risk neutral.
    • Boss cares about the profit π\piπ, not the effort eee.
  • Tom's payoff: v(w)−g(e)v(w) - g(e)v(w)−g(e)
    • Tom is risk averse: v(w)v(w)v(w) is increasing and concave, which means the marginal utility is decreasing as the wage increases.
    • g(e)g(e)g(e) is increasing, meaning higher effort is more exhausting.
    • Tom cares about the wage www, not the profit π\piπ for Boss.

Contract theory:

  • Principal: party wants a job done but cannot do it by himself
  • Agent: party that can do the job
  • Contract: terms determined by the principal about the agent doing the job
  • Optimal contract: incentivize the agent to do the job in the best interest of the principal

Optimal strategy:

  • Contract will let the Boss gets the highest expected payoff.
  • Tom will accept such offer only if his expected payoff is at least uˉ\bar{u}uˉ (uˉ\bar{u}uˉ: opportunity cost, the value of outside option).

Efficiency:

  • Boss is worse off if he wants to incentivize HHH and if effort is incontractible. Tom is indifferent.
  • In contrast, to incentivize LLL, whether effort is contractible does not matter.
  • Efficiency loss (due to “moral hazard”):
    • If if Boss chooses to incentivize high effort, the risk put in the wage scheme is pure waste.
    • When effort is incontractible, the additional cost to incentivize high effort may make Boss compromise on low effort, which he otherwise would not if effort were contractible.
