5.B Direct Mechanism

  • A direct mechanism has a flexible contract/menu.
  • The contract specifies two functions:
    • xxx: [0,V]→[0,1][0,V] \to [0,1][0,V]→[0,1] "allocation rule"
    • ppp: [0,V]→R[0,V] \to \mathbb{R}[0,V]→R "payment rule"
    • Denote Γ=D(x,p)\Gamma = D(x,p)Γ=D(x,p).
  • Mechanism:
    • Buyer decides to accept or reject the contract.
    • Buyer reports his type vvv if he accepts.
    • Buyer gets the car with prob x(v)x(v)x(v).
    • If buyer gets the car, buyer pays p(v)p(v)p(v) to seller.
  • Equilibrium (BNE - Bayes Nash Equilibrium):
    • Consider contract that are always accepted, D(x,p)D(x,p)D(x,p) becomes a simple one-shot game.
    • Condition 1 (no deviation): for buyer with vvv, EQ maximizes his expected payoff under vvv.
    • Condition 2 (acceptance): for buyer with vvv, EQ payoff is no less than 0 (outside option).
  • A special BNE: Truth-telling
    • σ(v)=v\sigma(v)=vσ(v)=v: buyer reveals his true type.
    • It is NOT always a BNE.
    • If it is, call D(x,p)D(x,p)D(x,p) is truth compatible.
